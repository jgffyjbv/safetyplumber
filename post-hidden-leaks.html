How to Spot a Hidden Water Leak

A slow, hidden leak can waste thousands of gallons and quietly damage your home long before you see a drip.

Not every leak announces itself. Many happen inside walls, under floors, or underground — where the only clues are subtle. Here is how to catch one early.

Watch for these signs

  • A water bill that climbs for no clear reason.
  • Damp spots, stains, or peeling paint on walls or ceilings.
  • A musty smell or unexplained mold.
  • The sound of running water when everything is off.
  • Warm spots on the floor (a sign of a hot-water line leak).

Try the meter test

Turn off every water fixture in the home, then check your water meter. Wait an hour without using any water and check again. If the reading changed, water is moving somewhere it should not be.

Why acting fast matters

Beyond the wasted water, a hidden leak can rot framing, ruin finishes, and feed mold. A licensed plumber can pinpoint the source — often without tearing open a whole wall — and repair it before it becomes a major expense.
